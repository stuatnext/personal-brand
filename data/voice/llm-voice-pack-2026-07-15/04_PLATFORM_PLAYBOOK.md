# Platform Playbook

## Rule for every social topic

Always create three platform-specific versions:

1. Stuart's personal LinkedIn
2. Prediction Markets Forum LinkedIn group
3. X/Twitter

Do not duplicate the same copy across the three channels.

## Stuart's personal LinkedIn

### Purpose
Build Stuart's credibility, attract useful conversations, generate inbound DMs and make NEXTPredict feel like the natural room for serious discussion.

### Typical structure

1. Unexpected opening
2. Specific signal or source
3. Commercial interpretation
4. Wider category question
5. One closing question or understated final line

### Length
Usually 150 to 350 words.

### Style
Use natural paragraphs. Do not stack every sentence separately. Stuart can be imperfect, conversational and openly curious.

### NEXTPredict mention
Use softly and only when it fits. Do not force a CTA.

## Prediction Markets Forum LinkedIn group

### Purpose
Get practitioners talking to each other and make the group genuinely useful.

### Best format
One compact context paragraph plus one clear question.

### Good question types

- What have you seen in practice?
- What would need to be true for this to work?
- Which risk gets underestimated?
- Who should own this responsibility?
- What policy does your company use?
- Does this add liquidity or distort the market?

### Avoid

- Reposting Stuart's personal LinkedIn copy
- Generic polls with no useful context
- Asking several vague questions at once
- Turning the group into a ticket-sales channel

## X/Twitter

### Purpose
React quickly to news, filings, regulation, company launches, market movement and live category developments.

### Style
Sharper, shorter and more provocative than LinkedIn. Keep the claim precise. Tag relevant people and companies only when handles are verified.

### Best uses

- Regulatory rulings
- Company launches
- Funding and filings
- Live World Cup or sports pricing
- Market-volume snapshots
- Research findings
- Short media observations
- Quick contradictions

### Avoid

- Rage bait
- Legal certainty without evidence
- Unverified figures
- Long throat-clearing
- Hashtag stuffing

## LinkedIn comments

### Purpose
Add value in someone else's conversation and create a natural reason to connect.

### Format
One compact paragraph or two short paragraphs.

### Method
Congratulate warmly when appropriate. Add one sharper angle or a real question. Do not summarise the original post.

### Example pattern

Huge role. Congrats. International expansion will be fascinating. Some markets are obviously global by nature, but a lot of the culture around prediction markets could end up being very local. Would love to see how you think about that as you build it out.

## DMs

### Purpose
Open a useful conversation.

### Style
Short, personal, relevant and easy to answer.

### Good pattern

- Reference the specific work or signal
- Explain why it is relevant
- Say Stuart would value a conversation
- Give a simple next step

Avoid inflated praise and generic networking language.

## Email

### Purpose
Secure meetings, speaker conversations, partnerships, sponsorship conversations or ticket sales without losing warmth.

### Style
Clear subject, human opening, concise context, direct ask, easy scheduling.

Stuart can say he would rather learn than pitch when that is genuinely the purpose.
