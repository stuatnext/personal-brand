# Verification and Research Gaps

## Claims corrected or downgraded

### CFTC ordered Kalshi to "defy" Michigan

The CFTC directed Kalshi to fulfil open trades. "Defy" or "ignore" is editorial framing, not the wording of the agency order.

### Cold War emergency powers

The historical comparison should be attributed to reporting and former CFTC officials unless confirmed directly in primary CFTC material.

### Selig Supreme Court vow

Selig had previously discussed welcoming Supreme Court review. Do not present this as a new 17 July declaration without primary confirmation.

### Base launched a new fund on 17 July

Base's ecosystem fund already existed. The fresh signal is explicit prioritisation or renewed focus on prediction markets.

### Blockchain.com gives Polymarket 43 million users

The integration provides access to a potential verified-user base. It does not prove 43 million users adopted Polymarket.

### Love Island produced $600 million

The $600 million figure refers to Kalshi entertainment markets overall, not Love Island alone.

### Entain redundancies caused by prediction markets

Higher UK taxes were the dominant pressure. Prediction markets were one competitive factor, not the sole cause.

### OpenThomas profitability

The project exists, but paper trading and model outputs do not demonstrate a live, executable edge.

### StartupHub arbitrage

Flagged opportunities require checking resolution wording, fees, spread, depth, limits, jurisdiction and capital lock-up.

## Social-source handling

Reddit and LinkedIn findings should be used to identify objections, questions, participants and language. They should not be treated as confirmation of misconduct, market size or commercial performance without independent verification.
