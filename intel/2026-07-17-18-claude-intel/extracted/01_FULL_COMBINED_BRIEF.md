# Prediction Markets Intelligence Brief

## Coverage: 17-18 July 2026

## Executive read

Prediction markets have entered the jurisdiction, integrity and infrastructure phase.

The strongest signals are no longer limited to trading volume. Federal and state authorities are issuing conflicting instructions. Congress is examining sports-market protections. France is blocking access while Malta explores a bespoke licensing route. Institutional firms are assembling specialist trading teams. Operators are internalising exchange infrastructure. New markets in entertainment, healthcare and corporate events are expanding the restricted-person universe and exposing weaknesses in settlement design, surveillance and data rights.

The commercial opportunity is growing alongside the burden of proof. NEXTPredict should place greater emphasis on market architecture, institutional trading, compliance infrastructure, resolution, data rights and political strategy. Generic category-growth discussions are becoming stale.

# 1. Highest-priority signals

## 1. CFTC intervention in the Michigan conflict

The CFTC stayed Kalshi's proposed emergency rule and directed the exchange to continue fulfilling open Michigan trades normally. The practical effect was to place Kalshi between a federal direction and a conflicting state-court position.

**Commercial read:** The dispute has moved beyond licensing and access. It now concerns whether a state court can disrupt transactions executed on a federally regulated derivatives venue.

**Content angle:** The CFTC has moved from defending its jurisdiction in court to intervening in the treatment of live trades. Kalshi appears caught between two authorities giving it incompatible instructions.

**NEXTPredict relevance:** Regulatory keynote, market-structure panel, enforcement and legal content.

**Priority:** 10/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 2. House Agriculture Committee hearing on sports prediction markets

The House Agriculture Subcommittee scheduled a 21 July hearing titled "Examining Customer Protections and Market Integrity in Sports Event Prediction Markets."

**Commercial read:** The hearing should produce named policy positions, witnesses, quotes and potential NEXTPredict speakers. It is more actionable than another broad regulation story.

**Recommended operation:**

1. Pre-hearing post: five questions Congress should ask.
2. Live monitoring: strongest claims, disagreements and witness positions.
3. Post-hearing analysis: who sounded credible, what remains unresolved and who belongs at NEXTPredict.

**Priority:** 10/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 3. France blocks Polymarket

France ordered internet providers to block Polymarket, escalating from restrictions on transactions to full access controls.

**Commercial read:** International expansion is becoming a country-by-country operational problem involving access, advertising, payments, classification and consumer protection.

**Content angle:** Can a prediction market build a genuinely international product when even the display of prices can be treated differently in each jurisdiction?

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 4. Malta explores a bespoke prediction-market framework

Malta is exploring a dedicated licensing approach that may sit outside conventional financial-services and gambling classifications. No final legislative text has been published.

**Commercial read:** Europe may be splitting into competing models. Some jurisdictions will restrict access under existing gambling law, while others may design new regimes to attract operators and infrastructure.

**NEXTPredict session:** Europe's Regulatory Fork: Block, License or Invent a New Category?

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** Medium-high

## 5. North Carolina adopts tax without direct sportsbook licensing

North Carolina enacted a 6% tax on prediction-market net trading-fee revenue, effective 1 January 2027, while recognising federal oversight rather than creating a conventional sportsbook licensing regime.

**Commercial read:** This creates a politically useful third model: accept federal regulation, avoid building a duplicate licensing structure and still collect state revenue.

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 6. Jump Trading expands its prediction-markets team

Jump reportedly doubled its specialist team to roughly 20 people and is recruiting from unconventional backgrounds including sports trading and independent forecasting.

**Commercial read:** Prediction markets are becoming a genuine institutional trading strategy rather than a side experiment.

**Lead:** Simon Johansen, Head of Prediction Markets.

**Priority:** 10/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 7. Moreton Capital builds a dedicated strategy

Moreton Capital Partners advertised for a senior Portfolio Manager to run a dedicated prediction-markets strategy using quantitative modelling, agentic AI and domain expertise. Reporting indicates it has already begun trading internal capital.

**Commercial read:** This is one of the clearest signs of dedicated institutional capital entering the category.

**Priority:** 10/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 8. Pascal raises capital for a trader-first venue

Pascal announced a $9 million Series A led by Union Square Ventures. Its proposition centres on APIs, order-book trading, professional interfaces and OTC capability.

**Commercial read:** The platform battle may shift from market count and consumer acquisition to which venue professional traders trust with meaningful size.

**Leads:** Ivo Crnkovic-Rubsamen and Matthew Downey.

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 9. DraftKings and Underdog internalise exchange infrastructure

DraftKings launched its proprietary DKeX exchange and reportedly received futures commission merchant approval. Underdog's UDX self-certified initial sports-event contracts with market-maker support.

**Commercial read:** Sports operators are moving from distribution partners to vertically integrated exchange businesses controlling customer acquisition, product, economics and infrastructure.

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** Medium-high

## 10. Blockchain.com embeds Polymarket

Blockchain.com integrated Polymarket into its brokerage experience for eligible users. Its reported 43 million verified users represent a potential distribution base, not confirmed prediction-market adoption.

**Commercial read:** Prediction-market distribution is moving into existing crypto, brokerage and financial interfaces.

**Priority:** 8/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 11. Kalshi and AppliedXL launch biopharma markets

Kalshi and AppliedXL launched a pilot covering clinical-trial outcomes and FDA decisions, initially focusing on late-stage trials after enrolment closes.

**Commercial read:** Health markets may create genuine information value, but they also multiply MNPI, ethical, patient-impact and restricted-person risks.

**Lead:** Francesco Marconi, AppliedXL.

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 12. Reality television becomes an integrity and brand-risk category

Coverage of Love Island and Survivor markets highlighted potential leakage, spoiler risk and the difficulty of controlling information across production staff, contestants, contractors and their networks.

Kalshi's total entertainment trading volume reportedly exceeded $600 million in 2026, up from $300 million in 2025. This figure is for entertainment markets overall, not Love Island alone.

**Commercial read:** Entertainment may be a powerful acquisition vertical, but it can also damage suspense and brand value before rights holders have developed policies.

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 13. Polymarket five-minute Bitcoin contracts show settlement-manipulation risk

Academic research found a repeatable pattern in which Binance spot order flow increased immediately before settlement of five-minute Polymarket Bitcoin markets, partially reversed afterwards, and benefited a concentrated group of traders. The same pattern was largely absent in fifteen-minute contracts.

**Commercial read:** Contract design and settlement windows can create manipulation incentives. A prediction market may be manipulated by moving the underlying reference market rather than trading directly on the prediction venue.

**NEXTPredict session:** How to Manipulate a Prediction Market Without Trading on It.

**Priority:** 10/10  
**Confidence:** High, subject to further peer review

## 14. SEC jurisdiction becomes more relevant

Contracts linked to public companies, issuer revenue, CEO statements, earnings, valuations or corporate outcomes may raise questions about security-based swaps or overlapping SEC jurisdiction.

**Commercial read:** The next regulatory conflict may involve federal overlap as well as federal-state pre-emption.

**NEXTPredict session:** When Does an Event Contract Become a Securities Product?

**Priority:** 8/10  
**Confidence:** Medium-high

## 15. Restricted-person identification is becoming a technology category

Robinhood surveillance leadership has described current processes for identifying restricted traders through occupations, employers and connections. The company is exploring AI assistance as contract variety expands.

**Commercial read:** Basic KYC is insufficient. Each contract creates a different potential insider population.

**NEXTPredict session:** Who Is an Insider When Every Contract Has a Different Insider List?

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 16. Data rights can determine whether a contract is launchable

Kalshi proposed using FlightAware as a primary resolution source for flight-cancellation contracts. FlightAware's parent RTX said it had not authorised the use and that its terms prohibited it.

**Commercial read:** Resolution-source permission, licensing, correction policies, backup sources and audit rights are part of product viability.

**NEXTPredict session:** You Cannot Settle What You Do Not Own: Data Rights Behind Event Contracts.

**Priority:** 8/10  
**Confidence:** High

## 17. PMXT is building a unified prediction-market API layer

PMXT is developing a CCXT-style interface across multiple prediction venues, including Python, TypeScript and MCP support.

**Commercial read:** Fragmented APIs become valuable infrastructure once firms trade across multiple venues and autonomous agents require standardised access.

**Priority:** 8/10  
**Confidence:** High for the project, low-medium for commercial traction

## 18. OpenThomas demonstrates autonomous weather-market trading

OpenThomas is an open-source agent designed to trade weather contracts using multiple forecasts, station-bias analysis, LLM adjustments and risk controls. Its default mode is paper trading.

**Commercial read:** Weather may be an early laboratory for autonomous agents because outcomes are frequent, objective and tied to known reporting stations.

**Caveat:** There is no evidence that the agent has a durable live-trading edge.

**Priority:** 7/10  
**Confidence:** High on project existence, low on commercial performance

## 19. Augur pivots toward resolution infrastructure

Augur's Lituus proposal focuses on decentralised resolution infrastructure that could support multiple prediction-market front ends.

**Commercial read:** The valuable layer may be resolution rather than end-user distribution.

**Freshness:** Strategic background, not a fresh 17-18 July launch.

**Priority:** 8/10

## 20. Entain cites prediction markets as competitive pressure

Entain announced cost reductions and role removals amid higher UK tax pressure. Reuters also reported prediction markets among the competitive pressures it is seeking to address.

**Commercial read:** Prediction markets are becoming a named strategic consideration for major incumbent operators.

**Caveat:** Do not claim prediction markets caused the redundancies.

**Priority:** 9/10  
**Confidence:** High
